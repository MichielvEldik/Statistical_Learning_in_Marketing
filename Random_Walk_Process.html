Random Walk Process

Imagine that we have a person that starts from origin (0), and will either go forward \(p\) or backward \(1-p\) based on a random probability. The walk is random in the sense that the future direction the person moves is random, and completely independent of anything in the present or past. Any previous actions say nothing about the next action. A random walk is not non-stationary because it isnt constant in mean, as is evident by our calculations. A .

Why Random Walk is not White Noise

No.For random walks, \(Var(x_{t+1}) = Var(x_t) + Var(\varepsilon_{t+1})\) is strictly increasing. With random noise, noise is constant.

Random Walk With Drift

Despite random walk being non-stationary, it retains the characteristic of having a constant mean. However, there is the possibility of random walk in a time series with a trend. In this case, drift helps to deal with that by adding a constant coefficient.
